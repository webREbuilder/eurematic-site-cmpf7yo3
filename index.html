How It Works

The privacy trilemma
finally solved.

On-chain health protocols need verified behavioral data to power token rewards and risk models. But requesting raw biometrics creates catastrophic privacy liability. Klara breaks this deadlock.

Before Klara
Protocols either forgo real data (enabling Sybil fraud) or ingest raw biometrics, creating regulatory exposure and user distrust.
With Klara
Protocols receive cryptographically verified attestations. Users' raw biometrics never leave their device ecosystem. Both parties win.
STEP 01
Wearable Data Collection

User's device (Oura, Whoop, Apple Watch) sends biometric streams to Klara's off-chain AI engine via encrypted SDK calls. Data stays fully off-chain.

STEP 02
AI + Differential Privacy Engine

Klara's AI evaluates wellness thresholds, applies calibrated noise (ε-differential privacy), and generates a cryptographic ZK proof of the achievement.

STEP 03
On-Chain Attestation

A boolean attestation is posted on-chain: "Wellness goal met: TRUE." Protocols trigger rewards, adjust risk models, or unlock features — with zero raw data exposed.

cryptographic pillars.

Differential Privacy (DP)

Calibrated Laplace/Gaussian noise applied to biometric inputs ensures mathematically bounded privacy guarantees under any threat model.

Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP)

Groth16 and PLONK proof systems generate succinct, EVM-verifiable proofs in milliseconds. Trustless verification without trusted setup requirements.
